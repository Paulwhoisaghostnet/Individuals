# Physical integration requirements

Physical integration turns devices into an installation. Enclosures, mounting,
cabling, and thermal behavior must support the conceptual arrangement while
remaining safe and serviceable.

Initial requirements:

- **PHY-001:** Displays, cameras, compute, and power components must have defined and
  independently serviceable mounting locations.
- **PHY-002:** No visitor-accessible edge, fastener, cable, or surface may create a
  foreseeable injury or entanglement hazard.
- **PHY-003:** Alignment between cameras and peer canvases must be repeatable.
- **PHY-004:** Signal and power paths must be labeled, strain-relieved, and
  replaceable.
- **PHY-005:** Enclosures must provide required airflow without introducing
  distracting sound or light.
- **PHY-006:** The installation must have a documented assembly, inspection, and
  disassembly sequence.

See `enclosures/`, `mounting-cabling/`, and `thermal/`.
